The Weight of Hope

Remind me of the days, long forgotten, when heart and mind moved as one, when a smile ruled my face and joy left no trace of doubt. Remind me how I loved her smile, how it caught me off guard, slipped into my being, teaching me what alive is. She gave me a reason— fragile, human, mortal— and I held it like a quiet prayer. Remind me how I confessed, with unarmored hope, expecting an answer because hope never plans for silence. Remind me of the nights I dreamed of her, how sleep pressed her name under my ribs, how hope throbbed weak and stubborn, but enough to remind me I was alive. Dreams held her there, a pulse lodged in my chest, silent, unyielding, teaching me how absence could feel like weight. Her response didn’t break me. It simply turned off the pulse, and the dark settled in the spaces she left behind. Remind me how I planned to move on, yet never did, clinging to a hope long gone, still scarred, still relentless.
