Midnight on a Nairobi Rooftop

Shawty at midnight, under moonlight, gorgeous 😍 Alas, only a flicker of hope clad in a fairy tale dream— on a Nairobi rooftop, where the city hums below like an endless heartbeat, yet I stand still, watching shadows stretch longer than love. The wind carries whispers, but none with your name. A candle of hope once, has dimmed prematurely, its smoke curls skyward, like prayers the night never answers. And days stretch longer than usual, each hour a weight, dragging memory through the marrow of my bones. Each minute like a thorn piercing my heart indifferently, yet I bleed in silence, for the city below would never notice my wounds. And that lady lost her phone and needs my help— so I wipe my tears quick, masking sorrow with borrowed courage, and ask, ‘Ulipoteza wapi?’ as if my heart wasn’t already lost too. We find it eventually, but at what cost? Her smile returns with the phone, but mine never does— for in giving back what was lost, I only felt the echo of what I cannot recover. Sad but real, maybe it's fate speaking silently through reality.
